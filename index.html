A brief introduction

Teachers
How are teachers at Waldorf?

At dusseldorf, kids are approached by well trained teachers who are well versed with abundant knowledge of how to nourish a pre-school kid. Teachers are trained to protect the child’s unconcious sensory organs. The classroom is well furnished appropraite to age. The classroom created by the teacher, the environment where the child can sense the world is good.
Teachers are all loving and the child to comfort with human care. Teachers are trained to be thoughtfully emotional to give a great deal to the educational process is a three dimenional way supporting the child’s body & mind.

Day at waldorf
How is a day at dusseldorf?

The day is programmed in a rythmic sense of day/week/month/year. All the activities encounter the rythm of nature to nourish the rythmic growth of physical body, soul & mind of the child. Actvities like the circle time, story time, indoor/outoor playtime, activity’/writing/drawing/painting/building/cleaning etc are designed to the rythm of life in nature.

Parents
Role of parents in dusseldorf

We educate parents in understanding the educational methods we practice to enhance the child development not only at school but also at home.Parental support for every child in a similar methodology simultaneous would help the up-bringing of the child without confusing the child while he is understanding the world. Parents are the integral part of the programme which is designed for the development of child.Special parenting programmes are designed which is must for every parent as a part of curriculum.Interactive sessiosn for parents/teachers/management is periodically designed as a part of curriculum.A wide spectrum of curriculum includes activities for kids/teachers/parents is to nurture the overall development of the child to build his respect for the natural world.

Assessment
How do you assess a child's progress

The child assessment starts from the child is taken for an admission at Duseldorf.Periodic assessment review will be discussed with the parents.Annual report of the child which includes the academic & overall development of the child.
